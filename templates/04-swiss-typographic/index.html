The Manifesto

We are taught to earn our own kindness. To arrive at the mat as a project to be corrected. We reject this entirely. Self-love is not the reward at the end of the practice — it is the ground you stand on to begin.

A.

Slow is the point

Our sequences are unhurried by design. We hold, we breathe, we notice. Depth over volume, always.

B.

Every body, present tense

No before-and-after. No goal weight, no perfect posture. The body you brought is the one we practice with.

C.

Rest is a discipline

We treat stillness as skill. Learning to stop is as rigorous — and as radical — as learning to move.

The Teacher

Elodie——
Fournier

Fifteen years of practice. One conviction: that the softest work is the hardest, and the most worthwhile.

Elodie came to yoga in recovery — not from injury, but from a decade of treating her body as a machine to be optimised. What she found on the mat was permission to stop. She has spent the years since building the kind of room she needed then: unhurried, unjudging, and quietly rigorous. She trained in Mysore and Berlin, teaches in French, English and German, and still practices every single morning before she teaches a soul.
