The studio

A warm room where you're allowed to be a beginner — forever.

Lumière began with a simple, slightly rebellious idea: that yoga could feel less like a workout and more like a Sunday morning. We stripped out the mirrors, dimmed the fluorescents, and filled the room with mustard cushions, trailing plants and the kind of music your favourite aunt played in 1974.

Every class is an invitation to soften — to meet your body where it is, not where an algorithm thinks it should be. Whether you can touch your toes is none of our business. How kindly you speak to yourself on the mat? That's the whole practice.

  • Body-neutral by design. No before-and-afters. No goals but presence.
  • Golden-hour lighting. Rooms lit like a 4pm daydream, all day long.
  • Small groups, big warmth. Twelve mats, one teacher who knows your name.

Your guide

Marisol Vega

Founder & lead teacher

Marisol trained across Oaxaca, Lisbon and a very leaky studio in Bristol before deciding the world had enough intense yoga and not nearly enough kind yoga. Fifteen years in, she teaches the way she wishes someone had taught her at twenty-two: slowly, warmly, and with a lot of permission to rest.

"Self-love isn't a bubble bath. It's the way you breathe when a pose gets hard — and choosing softness anyway."
